## Description

New Zealand government data and information management principles established in 2011, covering seven core principles: open, protected, readily available, trusted and authoritative, well managed, reasonably priced, and reusable. Defines how government agencies should manage data as a public asset with emphasis on accessibility, quality, stewardship, and lifecycle management.
